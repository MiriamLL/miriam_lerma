seamonas

A package with functions to make maps

1. Intro

seamonas provides easy access to information based on guidelines for monitoring seabirds at sea. It includes detailed table content and a sample dataset to support implementation. Information based on the HELCOM Monitoring Guidelines for Seabirds at Sea ESAS database.

About

seamonas contains:

  • A list of 549 species across 9 morphological separated groups.
  • Harmonized column names and provided detailed guidance for completing each entry.
  • Unified descriptions for Observations including abiotic structures, activity codes, associations.
  • one trip example from a monitoring containing basic trip information.
  • one survey example from a monitoring containing survey information.
  • one survey example from a monitoring containing species detected.

Installation

You can install the development version of seamonas from GitHub with:

devtools::install_github("MiriamLL/seamonas")

Start

Load the package

library(seamonas)

Key fields:

  • Code: Numerical code based on Euring. The species codes are primarily based on the EURING list, with additional ‘uncertainty codes’ commonly used by ESAS partners to represent species groups that are often difficult to identify under at-sea field conditions.
  • Scientific_name: A scientific name is the standardized, universally accepted name used to identify and classify living organisms. It follows a system called binomial nomenclature.
  • English_name: Official common name of the species.
  • Artificial_tax_class: A custom classification system based on taxonomic and morphological similarities among species.

Trip

seamonas contains:
- one trip example from a monitoring containing basic trip information.

data(Trip_test)

Field descriptions:

  • TRIP: Can be left blank or used for descriptions of the columns.
  • TRIP_ID: Sequential number of each survey day and any change in relevant fields.
  • Dataset_ID: Numerical identifier of the dataset in the database. Leave blank if unknown.
  • CRUISENO: Unique code identifying the survey.
  • OWPAS: Identifier for the contracting authority.
  • PROJECT: Identifier for the project.
  • CLUSTER: Identifier for the cluster or study area.
  • LAB: Name of the company conducting the survey.
  • SCIENTIST: Name of the responsible scientist at the surveying company.
  • DATATYPE: Type of data collected (e.g., Seabird Monitoring).
  • TECHNIQUE: Survey method used (e.g., video or still camera for digital surveys).
  • OBSERVER: Identifier of the surveyor or aircraft operator.
  • PLANE: Identifier of the aircraft used.
  • NUMBER_OF_PLANES: Number of planes used in the survey.
  • DOUBLE_PLATFORM: Define if survey include parallel flights (‘Yes’or ’No’).
  • DATE: Survey date in YYYYMMDD format.
  • STARTTIME: Time of start of survey in hh:mm:ss format.
  • STARTTIME: Time of the end of the survey in hh:mm:ss format.
  • CAMERA_SYSTEM: Description of the camera system used.
  • RESOLUTION: Numerical value of the resolution at sea surface.
  • PLANE_FLIGHT_HEIGHT_PLANNED: Numerical value of planned flight altitude.
  • STRIP_WIDHT: Numerical value of the width of the survey strip.
  • PLANE_SPEED: Numerical value for speed of the aircraft during the survey .
  • POSITION_ACCURACY: Mean deviation between recorded image position and actual GPS position.
  • METHOD_ID: Numerical value to define if the survey was made by 1= Transect or 2 = Grid.
  • AREA_OBSERVED: Numerical value of the area observed before processing the images.
  • AREA_ANALYSED: Numerical value of the area analysed after processing the images.
  • AREA_CONTROLLED: Numerical value of the area that was controlled during the screening.
  • ID_CONTROLLED: Numerical value of the total number of controlled objects.
  • SCREENING_CONTROL_DIFFERENCE: Percentage of the difference between screening and control.
  • ID_CONTROL_DIFFERENCE: Percentage of the difference between initial identification and audit.
  • QUALITY_IMPROVEMENT: Description of any quality improvement actions taken.
  • POSITIONSYSTEM: Specification of the positioning system used.
  • POSIT_PRECISION_CODE: Positional accuracy: 1 = decimal degree with 1 decimal place, 2 = decimal degree with 2 decimal places, 3 = decimal degree with 3 decimal places, and so on.
  • REFSYSTEM: Geodetic reference system used.
  • NOTES: Additional information not covered in previous fields.

Survey basis

seamonas contains:
- one survey example from a monitoring containing survey information.

data(Basis_test)

Field descriptions:

  • POSITIONS: Can be left blank or used for descriptions of the columns.
  • POSITION_ID: Unique numeric identifier used to link data between “Basisdaten” and “Observations”.
  • POSITION_ID_CONTROLLED: Numerical codes specifying if an image was controlled during screening. Includes values 1 to 3. Controlled during screening control (1), controlled during id control (2) and controlled both during screening and id control (3). Leave blank if not controlled.
  • Dataset_ID: Numerical identifier of the dataset in the database. Leave blank if unknown.
  • TRIP_ID: Sequential number representing each survey day and any change in relevant fields. This field is mandatory.
  • CRUISENO: Unique identifier for the survey. This field is mandatory.
  • CAMERA_NUMBER: Identifier for the camera used. This field is mandatory.
  • TRANSECT_NUMBER: Sequential number representing transect number, if the survey was conducted along transects.
  • DATE: Date of the survey in YYYYMMDD format. This field is mandatory.
  • TIME: Time of the image recording in hh:mm:ss format. This field is mandatory.
  • LAT_PIC_CENTER: Latitude of the image center, recorded in WGS 84 decimal degrees (six decimal places). This field is mandatory.
  • LON_PIC_CENTER: Longitude of the image center, recorded in WGS 84 decimal degrees (six decimal places). This field is mandatory.
  • PLANE_FLIGHT_HEIGHT: Recorded flight height of the aircraft at the time of image capture (in meters, ±10 m accuracy). Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • PIC_AREA_ANALYSED: Surface area of the image. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • GLARE: Numerical codes specifying sun glare intensity on the image, recorded at regular intervals Values expected are 0 = No glare; 1 = Low glare; 2 = Medium glare; 3 = Strong glare. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • SEASTATE: Numerical codes specifying sea state on the image, recorded at regular intervals. Values expected are 0 to 7. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • TURBIDITY: Numerical codes specifying water turbidity level based on visibility into the water, recorded at regular intervals. Values expected are 1 = No turbidity, 2 = Medium turbidity, 3 = Strong turbidity. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • ICE: Numerical codes specifying ice coverage, recorded at regular intervals. Usually 0 = No Ice. This field is mandatory.
  • CLARITY: Numerical codes specifying air clarity, recorded at regular intervals. Values expected are 0 = not recorded, 1 = Low clarity, 2 = Medium clarity, 3 = High clarity. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • PIC_QUALITY: Numerical codes specifying quality of the image, recorded at regular intervals. Values expected are 1 = Good quality, 2 = Bad quality. Some variation is expected. This field is mandatory.
  • PIC_FILENAME: File name structure prefered: Area_Date_PositionID_Code_Species_Camera_Modification.png. Example: A_20250616_ID30_6340_Common_Guillemot_c1_m1.
  • NOTES: Additional information not covered in previous fields.

Example of use

seamonas::Basis_test %>%
  summarise(GLARE_max=max(GLARE))
  GLARE_max
1         3

Observations

seamonas contains:
- one survey example from a monitoring containing species detected.

data(Observations_test)

Field descriptions:

  • OBSERVATIONS: Can be left blank or used for descriptions of the columns.
  • Observation_ID: Sequential number of each observation.
  • POSITION_ID: Unique numeric identifier used to link data between “Basisdaten” and “Observations”.
  • Dataset_ID: Numerical identifier of the dataset in the database. Leave blank if unknown.
  • CRUISENO: Unique identifier for the survey.
  • ABIOTIC_STRUCTURES: Numerical codes specifying physical abiotic features. Includes ships and infrastructures such as wind farms.
  • ABIOTIC_OBSERVATIONS: Numerical codes specifying abiotic non-living observations. Includes garbage, fishing lines, or any floating objects that are not animals (e.g., seaweed).
  • OBSERVATION: Numerical codes specifying the Euring code for every animal observation. Leave blank if the entry corresponds to ABIOTIC_STRUCTURES or ABIOTIC_OBSERVATIONS.
  • ENGLISH_NAME_BEFORE_CONTROL: English name assigned during initial identification. Should be a text field.
  • ENGLISH_NAME_CONTROL_ID: English name assigned during identification audit. Should be a text field. Leave blank if the observation was not audited.
  • ID_MATCH_MISMATCH: Numerical codes specifying identification consistency between initial identifier and auditor: match (1) or mismatch (2). Leave blank if not audited.
  • ENGLISH_NAME: English name assigned by the referee, corresponding to the Euring code. Leave blank if the entry corresponds to ABIOTIC_STRUCTURES or ABIOTIC_OBSERVATIONS.
  • DETECTION_STEP: Numerical codes specifying the detection stage: initial screening (1), control screening (2), both initial and control screening (3), initial identification, (4) audited identification (5). Leave blank if the position was not audited.
  • BEHAVIOUR: Numerical codes specifying animal behavior, e.g., holding fish, dipping, kleptoparasitizing.
  • ACTIVITY: Numerical codes specifying animal activity, such as: swimming (1), flying (2), submerged (3), breaching surface (4), associated with platform (5).
  • GROUP: Consecutively numbered groups; all members of a group share the same number. Leave blank if the animal doesn’t belong to a group.
  • FAMILY_GROUP: Groups consisting of adults and juveniles sharing the same number. Examples: Harbour porpoise with calf, Common guillemots with fledglings. eave blank if the animal doesnt belong to a family group.
  • ASSOCIATIONS: Numerical codes specifying animal associations with other animals or abiotic structures. Examples include Multi-species feeding associations (MSFA).
  • HEADING: Absolute movement direction of the animal in numeric compass bearings at 45° intervals (e.g., 0°, 45°, 90°, etc.).
  • SUBMERGED: Specification of the animal’s position relative to the water surface: breaching (o), below the surface (u) or unclear (x).
  • AGE_CLASS: Specification of the age category of the animal: adult (A) or immature (IM). May also include molting (x) or not molting (y).
  • AGE_YEAR: Numerical codes specifying the calendar year from 1 to 7.
  • PLUMAGE: Specification of the plumage type includes breeding (B), winter (W), and morphs.
  • SEX: Specification of the sex of the animal. Includes female (F) or male (M).
  • LENGTH: Linear body length measured from the tip of the beak to the tip of the tail in numerical.
  • WING_SPAN: Measurement across the full spread of the wings in numerical.
  • FLIGHT_HEIGHT: Approximate height of flight in flying birds.
  • FLIGHT_HEIGHT_CONFIDENCE: Confidence interval of height of flight in flying birds.
  • FLIGHT_HEIGHT_METHOD_ID: Numerical codes specifying the method for estimation of height of flight in flying birds. Categories include Parallax method (1), or calculated based on body size (2).
  • LAT_OBJECT: Latitude of the observation in exact coordinates recorded in WGS 84 format with six decimal places. Should not be left blank.
  • LON_OBJECT: Longitude of the observation in exact coordinates recorded in WGS 84 format with six decimal places.Should not be left blank.
  • NOTES: Additional information not covered in previous fields.
